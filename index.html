OSCOM is an optical tracking and characterization system designed specifically for responsive observation and analysis of small satellites and debris.

Its goal is to independently provide actionable information to spacecraft operators and engineers on the physical state of resident space objects.

OSCOM is a program of the Space and Atmospheric Instrumentation Lab at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University in Daytona Beach, FL.
